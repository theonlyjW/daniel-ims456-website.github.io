FACTORS









Human trafficking is influenced by various factors, including:



POVERTY

Economic vulnerability can make individuals more susceptible to trafficking, as they may be lured by promises of better employment and living conditions.



LACK OF EDUCATION

Limited access to education can contribute to vulnerability, as individuals may be less aware of the risks and more easily deceived.



GENDER INEQUALITY

Women and girls are often disproportionately affected, facing higher risks of exploitation, especially in the context of sexual trafficking.



POLITICAL INSTABILITY

Regions with political unrest or conflict may create an environment conducive to trafficking, with displaced populations at increased risk.



CORRUPTION

Corrupt systems can facilitate trafficking by allowing traffickers to operate with impunity, evading law enforcement.



DEMAND FOR CHEAP LABOR OR COMMERCIAL SEX

The demand for inexpensive labor and commercial sex services drives traffickers to exploit vulnerable individuals to meet these demands.



LACK OF LEGAL PROTECTIONS

Inadequate legal frameworks and enforcement can create an environment where traffickers face little consequence for their actions.
